TODAY’S UPDATES:
>	Asymptomatic passengers arriving from any of the COVID-19 affected countries should be under strict home quarantine for 14 days
-	Those who have come in contact with COVID-19 positive cases or come from high risk countries will be followed up through personal visit by health-workers
-	Passengers from all other COVID-19 affected countries will be followed through outbound calls through 104 call centre
>	All Symptomatic passengers who have visited any of the COVID-19 affected countries during last 14 days should be admitted at designated isolation hospital and tested for COVID-19
>	An orientation was held for medical college staff regarding COVID-19 infection prevention and control protocols and surveillance activities to support health department
>	As per Government of India instructions, quarantine of passengers from 7 countries (China, Italy, Iran, Korea, France, Spain and Germany) as per risk categorization has been initiated from 13th March 2020 onwards. All passengers of risk category 1 (Symptomatic on Arrival) and category 2 (aged above 60 years) are being quarantined at designated facility for 14 days from arrival in India
>	In view of increasing calls related to COVID-19, dedicated seating at 104 call centre has been increased to 40
>	One dedicated person from 108 will be present at 104 call centre to co-ordinate ambulance services
Appeal to Public: Any person who has returned from COVID-19 affected countries or has been in personal contact with such person must remain in home isolation for 14 days from arrival in India irrespective of having any symptoms or not. Also, should self-notify to nearest government hospital or call 104 health helpline. Maintain personal hygiene, use tissue paper/handkerchief while coughing or sneezing, frequently wash hands with soap and water or hand sanitizer and avoid attending mass social gatherings.
