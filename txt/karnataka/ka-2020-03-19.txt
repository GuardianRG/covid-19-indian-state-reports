World Health Organization has declared the recent Novel Corona virus, COVID-19 as Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) which has affected 166 countries/ territories including India till date. WHO has characterized COVID-19 as a Pandemic on 11th March 2020.
In this context, Karnataka state has strengthened all surveillance and containment measures against the possible spread of disease.

Today's Updates:
>	High level cabinet meeting was held yesterday under chairmanship of Hon'ble Chief Minister to review situation of COVID-19 in the state including actions and containment planning. Actions taken as below:
o A special state task force has been formed to review COVID-19 situation on a daily basis constituting Deputy Chief Minister Dr.CN.Ashwath Narayan, Health Minister Shri B.Sriramulu, Medical Education Minister Dr.K. Sudhakar, Home Minsiter Shri Basavaraj Bommai and Chief Secretary Shri TM Vijaya Bhaskar o Rs:200 crore rupees have been allocated for COVID-19 emergency response o All the precautionary measures announced on 13th March 2020 for a period of one week have been extended till 31st March 2020. Public are requested to co-operate in view of controlling further spread COVID-19.
>	A public notification has been issued to avoid visiting hospital for next 2 weeks for those coming for elective procedures, mild illness, follow-up care and outpatient care including dental patients at medical college hospital, district hospitals, super- specialty hospitals and autonomous institutions. Private hospitals are instructed to take similar steps to prevent crowding and spread of covid-19.
>	Home quarantine stamping for all international passengers has been initiated from 18th March 2020 at Kempegowda International airport-Bangalore and Mangalore International airport
>	A video conference meeting was held today with all first responder hospitals in the state to review preparations
104 ArogyaSahayavani (Toll Free Health-Helpline Call centre)
40 lines have been reserved for receiving calls of COVID-19. Till date,33,550 calls have been received for whom necessary guidance has been provided (3,181 calls received yesterday). 10 lines have been reserved for out-going calls where the employees of IT/BT and other companies who have returned from COVID-19 countries are being contacted regarding their health status.
Appeal to Public: Any person who has returned from COVID-19 affected countries or has been in personal contact with such person must remain in home isolation for 14 days from arrival in India irrespective of having any symptoms or not. Also, should self-notify to nearest government hospital or call 104 health helpline. Maintain personal hygiene, use tissue paper/handkerchief while coughing or sneezing, frequently wash hands with soap and water or hand sanitizer and avoid attending mass social gatherings.
