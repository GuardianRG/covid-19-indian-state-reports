NOVEL CORONAVIRUS (COVID-19) MEDIA BULLETIN
DATED: 18/03/2020
Situation till: 4:00 PM today
World Health Organization has declared the recent Novel Coronavirus, COVID-19 as Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) which has affected 159 countries/ territories including India till date. WHO has characterized COVID-19 as a Pandemic on 11th March 2020.
In this context, Karnataka state has strengthened all surveillance and containment measures against the possible spread of disease.
"Till date Fourteen COVID-19 positive cases have been reported in Karnataka state
including one death"
Thirteen COVID-19 positive patients are in isolation at designated hospitals and are stable.
Three New Positive confirmed cases have been reported today. Details as below;
o Patient 12: 25 year old male who is resident of Bangalore returned from Madrid-Spain on 13th March 2020. He is now admitted in designated isolation hospital. 7 primary contacts have been identified and are under strict home quarantine.
o Patient 13: 56 year old female who is resident of Bangalore returned from U.S on 6th March 2020 has tested positive for COVID-19. She is now admitted in designated isolation facility and stable. 50 primary contacts have been identified and are under strict home quarantine
o Patient 14: 35 year old male from Bangalore returned back from U.S on 10th March. He was on strict home quarantine. Got tested after developing symptoms on 16th March 2020. He has been confirmed COVID-19 positive and is admitted in designated isolation facility. Contact tracing is under progress
o Contact tracing of secondary contacts is under progress for all the cases.

Appeal to Public: Any person who has returned from COVID-19 affected countries or has been in personal contact with such person must remain in home isolation for 14 days from arrival in India irrespective of having any symptoms or not. Also, should self-notify to nearest government hospital or call 104 health helpline. Maintain personal hygiene, use tissue paper/handkerchief while coughing or sneezing, frequently wash hands with soap and water or hand sanitizer and avoid attending mass social gatherings.
