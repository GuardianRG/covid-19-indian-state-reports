Bulletin
Date: 22.02.2020
WHO has declared the recent Novel Corona Virus (COVID-19) epidemic affecting 30 countries as International Public Health Emergency of International concern. In this context, Government of West Bengal has strengthened the surveillance and control measures against the disease. As on today total 29790 travelers / persons have been screened at Kolkata & Bagdogra Airport and 28922 persons have been screened at seven Land Border Check Points along Nepal and Bangladesh. Also 1377 crew members of ships have been screened in three Ports. Till date 266 travelers from COVID-19 affected countries have been identified and placed under surveillance. For 14 of them the surveillance period has been over. Four are admitted in isolation facility on date. Rest 248 are under home surveillance. Till date 22 Samples have been sent to National Institute of Virology (NIV) Pune and ICMR-NICED, Kolkata for test of COVID-19. Results of all 22 samples have come as negative. The health condition of all the persons under surveillance is stable. A 24X7 control room is operating in the State Head Quarter. Isolation facilities with total 92 beds have been arranged in the following hospitals across the State: ID & BG Hospital, Kolkata, North Bengal MCH, RG Kar MCH, NRS MCH, IPGMER, Kolkata, Calcutta Medical College, Calcutta National Medical College, COM &SagarDatta Hospital, Imambara DH & Arambag SDH, Hooghly, Howrah DH & Satyabala ID Hospital, Howrah, Basirhat DH, Diamond Harbour GMCH, Kakdwip SDH, Rampurhat GMCH and Alipurduar DH.
In the background of the present COVID-19 outbreak, the general public is advised to adhere to health advisories issued by the State Government. All the guidelines for health and hygiene are posted on www.wbhealth.gov.in. People at large should follow the cough etiquette by covering the nose and mouth using handkerchief/towel while sneezing/coughing and do frequent hand-washing with soap and water. Those who have arrived from the COVID-19 affected countries should remain under home isolation for 28 days from the date of arrival in India, even if they do not have any symptoms. They should contact the Help Line no. given below in case they develop fever with cough or difficulty of breathing during this period.
