Appeal to Public
Any person with travel history to COVID19 affected countries or person with contact with such a traveler, whether they are having symptoms or not, must remain in home isolation. Avoid social greetings by touching, like hugs or handshake. Don't touch your face, nose and eyes. Use handkerchief while coughing/ sneezing. Use masks, only if you are taking a care of a person with suspected COVID-19 infection, or if you have symptoms. 'Healthy persons are not supposed to wear medical masks. Those who are wearing masks should know how to use and dispose it properly. N95 masks should not be worn by public.
Their self-isolation is the real prayer for the good of community, their family and themselves
Date: 09/03/2020
WHO has declared the COVID-19 epidemic affecting 102 countries as a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. Due to the inflow of persons from affected countries, Kerala state has strengthened the surveillance and control measures against the disease. 1116 persons are placed under surveillance, out of which 967 are under home isolation and 149 are admitted in designated isolation facilities. Till date 807 samples of suspect cases have been sent to the National Institute of Virology for testing out of which results of 717 samples came as negative.
Chief Secretary has requested the Cabinet Secretary in the National level video conference to delegate decision making at the State level so that central govt. offices and bodies take decisions and implement them fast. State govt. has also requested to increase the number of testing labs and to issue advisories regarding Kuwait and Saudi Arabia insisting of No Corona certificate for entry.
A 3 year old child, native of Kannur, who returned to Kerala from Italy on 07/03/2020 has been declared positive for COVID-19. The hospital authorities have confirmed that the health condition of all symptomatic persons under isolation in hospitals are stable.
In the background of the present COVID-19 outbreak, the general public is advised to adhere to health advisories issued by the State Government. Public should follow the cough etiquette by covering the nose and mouth using handkerchief/towel while sneezing/coughing. Do frequent handwashing with soap and water. Those who have
