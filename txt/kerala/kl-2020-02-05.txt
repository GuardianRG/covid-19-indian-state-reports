Strengthening Surveillance based on Evidence from Operational Research
WHO has declared the recent Novel Corona Virus (2019-nCoV) epidemic affecting
23 countries as International Public Health Emergency of International Concern. In
this context, Kerala state has strengthened the surveillance and control measures
against the disease. Till date, 2528 persons have been identified and placed under
surveillance, out of which 2435 are under home isolation and 93 are admitted in
selected isolation facilities. Till date 223 Samples of suspect cases have been sent
to National Institute of Virology for testing out of which results of 193 samples came
as negative. The health status of all students who were tested positive for Corona
Virus remain satisfactory. The hospital authorities have confirmed that the health
condition of all symptomatic persons under isolation in hospitals are stable. A 24x7
control room has been set up in the State and all District Head Quarters. A minimum
of two hospitals with isolation facilities have been identified in each district. Sample
testing for nCoV at NIV, Alappuzha Unit is functional, and samples are being tested
In the background of the present nCorona Virus outbreak, the general public is
advised to adhere to health advisories issued by the State Government. Public

Complimentary Activities
•	The isolation status of persons who returned from Wuhan, China and now under surveillance evaluated. 30% of those under isolation were following isolation instructions excellently, 54% were found to be satisfactory and 16% were found to be poor. Proper isolation Instructions were given to the persons having poor isolation status.
•	Single window communication channel established with all district control rooms for high priority communications, headed at the state by state control room. Automated data capturing formats for real time data capturing from districts established.
•	Human resource management teams have been constituted at the state and district levels. Adequate HR has been ensured for isolation, airport surveillance, transport, isolation services, contact tracing and call centre
•	Sample management team has ensured the supply of 200 Viral transport media (VTM) to the districts from the State Public Health Lab today
•	A training monitoring unit has been formulated at the State and district levels for content development and capacity building
•	Training for ASHA workers completed in 8 districts
•	Training given to 1293 Elected representatives, 2544 Kudumbashree volunteers, 206 other volunteers and 243 staff from other departments
•	Adequate infrastructure facilities ensured at all designated isolation facilities.
•	For providing psychosocial support to the families of suspected persons 191 persons have been deployed across the state. 1696 tele counselling services were provided till date.
