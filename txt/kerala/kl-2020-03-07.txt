Dedicated to People under Home isolation
In view of new cases being reported and community spread in Italy and Iran, Govt, of India has instructed to screen passengers coming through direct flights from Iran & Italy in addition to China, Hong Kong, Thailand, Singapore, Japan, South Korea, Vietnam, Nepal, Indonesia and Malaysia. All people coming from the affected areas of these countries and already notified countries should follow the advisory. People coming from Republic of Korea, Iran and Italy or having such travel history since 10th February 2020 may be quarantined for 14 days on arrival to India. Asymptomatic persons should remain in home isolation for 14 days. Symptomatic persons should contact the concerned designated isolation facilities.
WHO has declared the COVID-19 epidemic affecting 89 countries as a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. Due to the inflow of persons from affected countries, Kerala state has strengthened the surveillance and control measures against the disease. 637 persons are placed under surveillance, out of which 574 are under home isolation and 63 are admitted in designated isolation facilities. Till date 682 samples of suspect cases have been sent to the National Institute of Virology for testing out of which results of 616 samples came as negative. The hospital authorities have confirmed that the health condition of all symptomatic persons under isolation in hospitals are stable.

In the background of the present COVID-19 outbreak, the general public is advised to adhere to health advisories issued by the State Government. Public should follow the cough etiquette by covering the nose and mouth using handkerchief/towel while sneezing/coughing. Do frequent handwashing with soap and water. Those who have arrived from the COVID-19 affected countries should remain under strict home isolation as per the revised guidelines issued on 19/02/2020.
